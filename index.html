About Nathaniel

Hailing from the land of drift wood, grow ops and beastly ravens comes a bilingual french canadian 90's child.

As an engineering student he built devices that aid those with physical disabilities, he launched an on-going student team to build research satellites, and he contributed to the improvement of mankinds ability to peer more clearly into deep space.

He resides in Montreal since 2012 where he has worked on two large hospital design projects with the large engineering firm SNC-Lavalin doing primarily energy flow analysis and computational fluid simulations.

When he left the firm in 2014, he broadened his field of interests taking up pottery and slacklining, and travelled abroad to go rock climbing in south east asia where he also explored the philosophies of buddhism at various mediation retreats. Today he is actively engaged with the concept of open value networks at Sensorica, and exploring the technological basis for decentralized applications.

Professional Experience

Did energy consumption and loss estimates for CUSM hospital project during internship in 2010. In 2012-2014, did wind, smoke and fire simulations as well as project-wide energy flow and heat exchange analysis for CHUSJ hospital project.

UVic Adaptive Optics Lab RAVEN project

After launching and leading UVic's ECOSat team for a year, the adaptive optics laboratory was an opportunity to learn a great more deal about space and our relationship to it while working on a state of the art, science instrument which is today installed at the Subaru telescope in Mauna Kea, Hawaii. I got to be the entire production line of several components of the instrument, from development to manufacturing to quality testing to installation.
